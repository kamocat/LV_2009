the process:

 

to calculate a team's valubility, we use several arrays of data:

 
     
  • the teams that participated on the Red and Blue alliances in each match
    •  
  • the scores of the Red and Blue alliance for each match
    •  
  • the indexes of all matches each team participated in (when dealing with only one regional, this index is the match# -1) This match index
    •  
 

A team's offensive and defensive valubility is initially calculated by averaging their alliance scores in each match (the offensive value) and averaging the opposing alliance scores in each match (the defensive value), and then dividing both by three (the number of teams on an alliance). That means the offensive (benefit) is how many points that team tends to score, and the defensive (detriment) is how many points tend to be scored on that team.

 

From there, it's an iterative process. For each match, the team's valubilities is recalculated by the proportion of its previous valubilities to the valubilities of the two other teams on the alliance. The offensive valubility is multiplied by the alliance's score, and the defensive valubility is multiplied by the opposing alliance's score. The drift of valubilities seems to slow down to less than 0.01 per iteration by the 150th iteration. (While the valubilites are calculated in floating point, they are saved as integers, because it's decreases complexity to save all data as one datatype, and it's misleading to post the team numbers as floating point). All posted results have been calculated to the 150th iteration or greater.
